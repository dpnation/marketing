Work Receipts: Building a self-service record of impact

How a one-person product marketing function used Claude to solve a reporting problem that scaled with her AI tool ecosystem - not just her manual workload.

What it is

Work Receipts is a Claude Project built to solve two connected problems for a one-person product marketing team: work gets logged in Monday.com, but a Monday connector isn't available in Claude under current IT policy, so there's no way to automatically roll up work spanning Highspot, GitHub, Slack, and email into one summary - and Monday itself can't calculate percentage completion against quarterly MBOs. That left quarterly performance reporting to be reconstructed from memory under deadline pressure.

How it works

The receipt didn't stop at the time saved - it also flagged what still needed human verification before the report went anywhere near external stakeholders: a couple of stats with source discrepancies, and a technical claim that needed IT confirmation before it could be cited. The tool sped up the work; the receipt kept the shortcut honest.

Why she built it

  • No automated rollup existed for her tools and workflows, so manual tracking was either skipped under time pressure or reconstructed inaccurately at reporting time.
  • She wanted defensible proof points tied to her actual goals, not just a list of activities - real time-saved estimates and business impact framing.
  • As a team of one running a growing AI tool ecosystem, she needed a system that could recall work from early in a reporting period, not just whatever was top of mind recently.
  • Her AI projects rarely ship as one-and-done - they evolve from a rough v1 into more robust, more complicated versions over the course of a quarter, and sometimes across several. She needed a way to account for that evolution at each phase, not just log the final state.

How it's been tweaked

  • From append to replace. The original approach logged each new deliverable as a separate memory entry. This got unwieldy fast - the system now maintains a single consolidated running tally per period, updated by replacing the entry rather than appending to it, to keep it scannable and prevent duplication or drift.
  • Human confirms before it's logged. Each receipt is confirmed before it's committed to memory - nothing gets logged unilaterally.
  • A built-in "don't oversell" guardrail. When work is complete but not yet live - for example, content built but publishing after a reporting deadline - the system defaults to conservative framing over an inflated one, even when a more generous claim would be arguable.

Demonstrated impact

Over one full quarter, the system consolidated more than a dozen shipped AI tools, the GEO/AEO measurement suite, several analyst relations engagements, an originated industry webinar, and a cross-functional adoption signal (another team independently adopted one of her tools) into a single, structured performance report - with the reasoning behind each attainment claim documented, not just asserted.

What's next

  • A Monday connector, if it becomes available in Claude, would help with rolling up a quarter's worth of logged work automatically. But the connector alone wouldn't replace this system - the summarization, the reasoning behind a percentage-completion call, and the MBOs themselves (which don't live in Monday) would still need to come from Claude. A connector is a project enhancement, not a reason for Work Receipts to go away.
  • The same receipt structure is already being extended into the newer Competitive Intel project - an automated weekly discovery-scan pipeline is being logged the same way.
