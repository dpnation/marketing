Findings & Actions

7 Actions
P1

Update H1 to "About Applause" (or a keyword-bearing variant).

The browser title, og:title, meta description, and breadcrumb all use "About Applause." The H1 is "Release Faster, With Confidence." One copy edit — the fastest H1 fix in the batch. Optional upgrade: "About Applause — Crowdtesting Pioneer and Leader in Managed Software Testing" combines the brand name with keyword-bearing context.

P1

Add Organization schema with full entity attributes.

This About page is the canonical location for Applause's Organization schema. It should include: name, description (the opening brand statement), url, headquarters address (Boston), additional office locations, investor (Vista Equity Partners), CEO (Chris Malone), founding claim ("crowdtesting pioneers"), number of community members (1.5M+), and knowsAbout attributes. Starter template below. This is the single highest-impact schema action across all product pages in the batch — it builds the entity model AI systems use to describe Applause sitewide.

P1

Add award data as text in the HTML — not just in image alt text.

The four award categories (Stevie, SD Times, Tech Breakthrough, USA Today) are confirmed on the page, but their names and years appear primarily in image filenames and alt text rather than in body HTML text. Add each award as a visible text element beneath the award logo: "[Organization] — [Award Name] — [Years]." This makes the award data indexable and extractable without relying on image alt text parsing. Award schema (via award property on the Organization) can then reference this confirmed text content.

P2

Schema-tag the CEO quote.

Chris Malone's quote ("Today, brands win or lose based on their digital experiences…") is a named executive statement with full title confirmation. Adding a Person schema block for Chris Malone (with jobTitle: "Chief Executive Officer" and worksFor: Applause) reinforces the entity model. The quote itself can be referenced via the Statement type. Minor but contributes to overall entity recognition.

P2

Add inline outcome metrics for the three customer success stories.

Western Union, Intermarché, and Dow Jones are named, but each description is a single generic sentence with no outcome metrics. Adding one quantified result per customer — e.g., "Western Union: payment testing across 200+ markets and 130+ currencies" — gives AI systems a citable outcome fact, not just a brand name association.

P2

Make the founding/pioneer claim explicit and dated.

The page says "crowdtesting pioneers" without a founding year. Adding "Founded in 2009, Applause pioneered the crowdtesting model…" gives AI systems a chronological anchor for the brand history. This is the type of specific founding fact Wikipedia and AI entity models use to describe company histories. Confirm the founding year with the team before adding.

P3

Add a brief "What is Applause?" definition block.

The opening brand statement is strong but buried after the H1 in a paragraph. Adding a single sentence before it — formatted as a clear definition: "Applause is a managed software testing company and the pioneer of crowdtesting, providing enterprises with real-world testing at global scale." — gives AI systems a clean, schema-friendly definition to extract for "what is Applause?" queries.
