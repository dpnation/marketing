Executive Summary

Action Required — H1 Is Invisible to AI
⛔ Critical Issue — H1 Is "OUR SOLUTIONS"
The page's H1 is literally the words "OUR SOLUTIONS" in all caps. This is the worst heading in the audit batch. AI systems use the H1 as the primary signal for what a page is about — "OUR SOLUTIONS" matches no query anyone has ever typed. It provides no category signal, no keyword, and no extractable value. This single change — rewriting the H1 to match how buyers search — is the highest-leverage action on the entire page.

This is the first product hub page in the audit batch, so several blog-post checks don't apply (no author, no date, no HowTo schema). The relevant standard shifts: a solutions catalog page should tell AI systems what Applause offers, who it's for, and what each solution does — in extractable, structured form. Currently it does none of these things effectively. The opening is 200 words of dense marketing prose before a single solution is named. The 9 listed solutions have descriptions of 1–2 sentences each — too short to be cited as standalone answers. Security Testing is missing from the catalog despite appearing in the navigation. And there are no proof points anywhere on the page.

Why This Matters
A solutions catalog page is one of the clearest schema opportunities in the entire site. ItemList schema explicitly tells AI systems "this page contains a list of named services offered by Applause." Service schema on each item adds the category, description, and URL. Together, they make this page the machine-readable source of record for "what does Applause offer" — which AI systems currently have to infer from scattered mentions across the site.
