Competitive Gaps

Why this matters: The vendors AI engines cite for this query space aren't the ones in Applause's standard competitor set — they're a distinct category of specialist data/annotation firms, several with far bigger, more recent AI-specific client rosters.
  1. Scale AI / Surge AI — own the "premium frontier-LLM" framing; cited with named clients (OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, Google) and specific valuation/scale figures. Applause counter: lean into "managed program, not marketplace" — a real structural difference from Scale's self-serve platform model — but this argument isn't made anywhere on the page today.
  2. TELUS International (Lionbridge) / Appen / Centific — win on multilingual breadth (170–235+ countries/dialects) with the exact same "world's largest community" framing Applause uses. Pure scale claims won't differentiate Applause here since three competitors make near-identical claims. The credentialed-specialist angle (clinicians, attorneys, financial pros) is Applause's most ownable differentiator on this page and is currently under-emphasized relative to the generic scale stats.
  3. Cogito Tech / iMerit / Sama — win on named enterprise AI clients and quantified project volume (e.g., "10,000+ projects," "60M+ AI elements"). Applause's anonymized case studies can't compete with this level of specificity for an AI-buyer audience doing vendor diligence.

Handoff: recommend a dedicated competitive-intelligence pass on Scale AI, TELUS International, and Cogito Tech specifically for the AI data collection / RLHF category — these are not currently in the tracked competitor set and should be added for this content line.
