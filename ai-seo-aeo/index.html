The Framework

Three pillars, one skill

The AI SEO / AEO skill applies a three-pillar framework to any Applause content — whether you're auditing existing pages, optimizing a blog post, or building net-new comparison content.

🏗️

Structure

Make content extractable. AI systems pull passages, not pages. Every key claim should work as a standalone statement — definition blocks, comparison tables, FAQ sections, step-by-step answers.

🏅

Authority

Make content citable. AI prefers sources it can trust. Include statistics with sources, named expert quotes, author credentials, and "last updated" dates. The Princeton GEO research shows citations alone boost visibility +40%.

📡

Presence

Be where AI looks. Your website is one input. AI also cites Wikipedia (7.8% of ChatGPT citations), Reddit, review sites, and industry publications. Allow AI bots in robots.txt — block them and you can't be cited.

Best Practices

Getting the most out of it

Patterns that make all the difference.

🔍

Audit before you build

Run the AI Visibility Audit before creating new content. Optimizing an existing page that's close to ranking is almost always faster than building net-new.

📊

Lead with the 50% stat

The "50% reduction in defects released to production" stat is your most citable proof point. Build it into every piece of content the skill produces — with attribution.

⚡

AI testing = content gap

Testlio and GAT are behind on AI testing content. The "AI testing tools for enterprises" and "how to test AI applications" queries are open lanes — move fast.

🔄

Freshness is a ranking signal

ChatGPT cites content updated in the last 30 days 3.2x more often than older content. Add "Last updated" dates to competitive pages and refresh them monthly.

🏗️

Comparison pages are highest-ROI

Comparison content drives ~33% of all AI citations. "Crowdtesting vs in-house QA" and "Applause vs Testlio" pages should be priority builds.

🤖

Check robots.txt first

If GPTBot, ClaudeBot, or Google-Extended are blocked in your robots.txt, no optimization matters — those platforms can't cite you. This is the first thing to check.
